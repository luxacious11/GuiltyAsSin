Abilities
Kitsune Phsyiology

Form Shifts

The one thing Chris has managed to master is shifting between fox, kistune, and human. He is capable of partial shifts between any of these forms. His fox form is a melanistic red fox, while his kitsune form is the same but multiple tails. Currently, he has seven. While in either form, or partially shifted, his senses match that of the fox instead of human.

Chaos Manipulation

The only portion of this ability that Chris has control over is small sparks of electricity. They don't do much unless there's something else around to interact with them - like anything particularly dry - but there was always hope he would garner more control and variety over time. In theory, this is true, but the method through which he was taught was not appropriate for him and now he's shut himself off from it subconsciously, so any progress is impossible at this time. Unbeknownst to him, it does come with a few small perks, though, in that he is able to sense oncoming danger and disaster, as he is more attuned to the chaos in the world around him than he realizes.

Immortality

The only thing he never had to work at, Chris ceased aging in his thirties and is largely immortal. He heals faster and from more serious injuries than most. He is also incapable of getting sick.

Fox Fire

He is currently incapable of summoning any fox fire.
Limitations
Kitsune Phsyiology

Form Shifts

His senses only match that of the shifted body part; as a full human, he does not have improved senses in the slightest. He has been known to display his tails when under extreme duress or other strong emotions, even now while he's older, and he does occasionally shift between forms in his sleep without meaning to.

Chaos Manipulation

His lack of control over this power, save the small pieces he knows he's capable of, means that if and when any additional abilities manifest, they are likely to do so extremely destructively. As of now, his abilities in this regard are so minimal that their weakness in and of themselves is his limitation.

Immortality

While unaging and difficult to kill, Chris can still be killed via instantly-fatal wounds or prolonged suffering (such as oxygen deprivation, fire, etc). He likes to think he doesn't need to sleep as part of this, but that's a lie - he's just more resilient to death through lack of sleep than the average person.

Fox Fire

He cannot currently summon this.

About
  • Chris has gone by many names in the past. Usually, he picked variants on his first name - different spellings, different abbreviations - and then entirely new last names. He no longer remembers what it was, originally - just that it wasn't Reeves.
  • He took after his mother in almost every way, not that he knows this. He was the product of rape, ultimately, which was when his mother ran - leaving him behind because his father had gotten what he wanted: an 'heir' to take after him. Unfortunately for Chris, he was simply too much like his mother to appease his father. While able to shift to fox form and call on some electricity (now - and even that took hundreds of years to garner), Chris was altogether incapable of most things his father expected of him when he took after the man in the powers department.
  • In his attempts to teach Chris and draw out his kitsune-type abilities, his father would typically resort to corporal punishment. Chris's body bears the marks of this to this day, as it went on for a little over four hundred years before he turned and gained the ability to heal. They're all old and somewhat faded - at least, the ones that aren't raised from scar tissue - but they're still a point of contention. They remind him of that helpless feeling where he wasn't - and never would be - good enough, and they remind him of what he did to escape that (however accidental it was), and so he has yet to truly accept them and avoids mirrors unless clothed as a result.
  • Although a scholar at heart, Chris is capable of extreme violence - he learned something from his father, after all - but he does his best to avoid it. This is exactly contrary to his reputation. To avoid being looked into too heavily - and to avoid anyone else tracking him down - he pretends the metahumans he smuggles to safety are victims of human trafficking. He hires from the depths of criminal circles, including from others involved in trafficking of humans, weapons, and artifacts, and he pays them handsome fees in exchange for their discretion, silence, and loyalty. In return, when they want to retire, he sets them up with substantial funds and fakes their deaths in horrible, gruesome ways to perpetuate his reputation. He has yet to kill even once, save his father, in his life, but- well. Law enforcement sure as hell think otherwise.
  • Honestly, he just wants to be left alone?
  • This does mean, however, that he has a lot of connections throughout the world, as he moves his primary base of operations around every few years.
  • Seriously, he can and will get you names of restaurants, vineyards, and so on in all the major ports of call around the world - and he's on good terms with all of them. They may not be five star establishments, but they do still make room for him whenever he pays a visit. They're ex-employees, all of them, and they owe him that much - even if he wouldn't say they owed him at all.
  • He has residences in a variety of countries, all carefully closed up, maintained, and ready for re-inhabiting should they be needed. They're often used as safe houses for the individuals he helps, and he keeps meticulous records of everything so that, in the worst case scenario, if he ever does land himself in some hot water, he can also get himself out. This includes video and photographic proof of the care provided - top notch - and statements from the individuals he helps that they hired him.
  • He settled just outside Limbo shortly after the Serval facility burned down, but he's passed in and out of the area before. Contacts here were who informed him that the facility was an illegal testing site for metahumans, which prompted the move into the area.
  • He's damn good at putting on a show of being charming and unshakable - arrogant, even - but he's nowhere near it. Charming, yes, absolutely, but at his core he's still that terrified little boy who was never good enough. Someday, he's going to crack and realize it - but fuck if anyone knows when.
  • He was born in what is considered the High Middle Ages in what was then termed the Gaelic Kingdoms - that is, Gaelic Ireland. He's long since lost the accent in everyday speech, but his first language is Gaelic. He's multi-lingual, now, obviously, having picked up the majority of major languages in the areas he's been, including English, French, Italian, Spanish, and Greek. He has others, but these are his strongest - the rest... it's been a while, to put it mildly.
  • Although not particularly religious, he spent a good portion of his life in a monastery in the Alps. During this time, he did not speak, but he was responsible for a lot of additions to their library and their wealth of knowledge. He still returns to visit every few years, or when he feels lost, and his room still remains for him.

Overview
You would think a kitsune-type metahuman would have more power than one person should ever wield, what their the tendency towards chaos manipulation, but it's not so simple for Chris. He never developed his powers, unfortunately - at least, not any particularly strong ones - and was left instead as a disappointment to the only person whose praise he ever wanted: his father. This led to centuries of, well, torture before Chris ended up killing the very man whose acceptance was all he'd ever hoped for. It was an accident, of course, but that hasn't alleviated the guilt that's followed Chris through life since, even as he threw himself into helping others like him in the only way he knows how- by pretending he's not so that most people look the other way in favour of not winding up dead. He might give off an air of confidence, even cockiness, but inside he's far more broken than he'd ever care to let on.
