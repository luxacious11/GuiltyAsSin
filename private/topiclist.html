    The Dockyards
    Something Inside Written By Sara Darling Yesterday at 09:03 pm
    The original hub of activity for the entire city, the Dockyards is where most of the oceanic transit in and out of the city takes place. Ships come in to be unloaded, or are stocked up and depart to carry their goods further south. This area is primarily commercial, with only meager apartments to be found, usually reserved for the ship workers who spend long hours and late nights on the docks.

    Carnivale
    Written By --
    An amusement park that’s been threatening to close its doors for as long as anybody can remember, Carnivale is hardly thriving, but it’s somehow limping along nevertheless. This has become a popular attraction for kids and teenagers alike, whether young children dragging along tired parents or young couples on their very first dates. There are rides, carnival games, and plenty of eateries to spend your money at - just be sure to keep an eye on your pockets, because some less-savory types frequent the fairgrounds as well.

    The Graveyard
    one woman army Written By Connor Avery January 3 2025, 12:12 AM
    The northernmost expanse of the Dockyards has come to be known as the Graveyard, as decrepit and disused as it is. Seacans sit empty, piers degrade into the ocean, and shadows fill the space between everything with a sense of danger that makes this a popular spot for more clandestine meetings- especially ones of a more criminal nature.
